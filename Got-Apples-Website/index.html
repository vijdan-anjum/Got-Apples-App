Vision STATEMENT

You got ingredients? WE GOT FOOD..The Got Apples will be a simple and convenient tool used to show recipes to anyone who wants to cook but are not sure what to cook with the ingredients that they have on hand or would like to use.

Description of intended USER

The Got Apples can be used by anyone who is at least 14 years old, an age where you would be comfortable to use kitchen utensils. These individuals can range from anyone who does not know how to cook, to cooks that have no idea what to eat for the day, to cooks who want to try out some professional dishes made by a professional chef but want to do so in the comfort of their own home.

Amazing Features and Services

01

Shows Ingredient Suggestions

Accept any ingredients that a cook wants to use or ingredients that they have and shows the suggestions of the name of ingredients while user is adding the ingredient name to make the desired ingredient list(suggestions are based off the ingredients already stored in the app that are used in any of the recipes stored)

02

Clear Display of the Added Ingredients

Ingredients that user has added can be clearly viewed as a list on the same screen as to where they are adding it

03

Easy Deletion of Ingredients

Easy deletion of individual ingredients if user does not want to use it anymore once they have added it to the ingredient list

04

Simple Selection of Cuisines

Choose any one cuisine type such as American, Italian, Indian, etc., to view recipes of that cuisine category or simply select ALL to view recommendations of recipes from all the cuisine types that are possible to cook with the ingredients added

05

Lucid Way of Selecting and Viewing Recipes

Name of the recipes possible to cook with the added ingredients and selected cuisine type are viewable in a systematic manner which will make user to easily select the recipe of their choice and then view that particular recipe itself mainly including its ingredients and procedure

06

Share recipes

Recipes can be shared

    Why will someone want to install our software?

  • Convenience and Simplicity

    We value convenience and simplicity that is valuable to everyone, since eating is a necessity for all of us. Our app mainly achieves this because of its main idea of INGREDIENTS to RECIPES, that is, it goes from cooks knowing available ingredients to finding recipes of their choice, rather than having to know the cuisine and/or the recipe name first

    Support you to avoid wastage of food items available with you since we recommend recipes even if one or two ingredients are missing from your ingredients list

  • Reduced Stress

    Help in reducing your stress around thinking about what to cook today for your meal.

  • Tried New Cuisines

    Initiate you to try different cuisines which you might have not even think of trying.

Postmortem

    What went right in the development process?

  • ----------------------

    After handing in Iteration 2, our team reflected on the things we did right, and things that we did wrong. We communicated regularly throughout each iteration which was one of the assets of our team. This made our beginning phase, that is, iteration 0 very well discussed and systematic. But in the consecutive iterations, we believe our team got a little overwhelmed with learning GitLab, Android Studio, and other things while maintaining the organizational aspect of the project, that is, documentation and regular discussions using the GitLab itself.

    • What went wrong in the development process?

  • ----------------------

    In Iteration 1's retrospective, our main problem was not utilizing GitLab’s organizational features to their full potential, so we decided to utilize GitLab even further for Iteration 2. While we did that in Iteration 2 and we were organized in terms of who is doing what task, we still did not close down issues in time as we were going back-and-forth on Discord; which was great in terms of effective communication in the given instance but not quite effective closer to the few days before the release. So, while we were focused on task organization, we ended up losing track of time organization and load management. Also, we reflected that our division of tasks by architecture classifications did not exactly work as planned. Basically, one of us handled persistence classes, one handled objects and logic classes, one handled user interface, and one handled documentation and testing. This proved to be harder than anticipated since they all correlate and relate, which in turn ended up not making a feature work when one of the classes did not work.

    • What would we do differently if we had the chance to start over?

  • ----------------------

    Therefore, we all agreed that for Iteration 3, we should close every task that we finish, divide the tasks into even smaller tasks and assign every task between us, four developers, while focusing on one feature at a time and fully implementing it. In addition, this time we also considered fixing bugs from the previous iteration and additional documentation tasks required for this iteration as a separate set of tasks so that each task can be given equal value and consideration explicitly. This resulted in better overall management of tasks as compared to previous iterations. Thus, if we had a chance to start over differently, we would try to minimize the little procrastination that we did this time & try our best to utilize GitLab to its full potential for organizing. We all also considered this as a good wake-up call to realize that we are a little disorganized as students and could have reflected on that little earlier.

    The image on the right is a graph showing our project's velocity of Iteration 1, Iteration 2, and Iteration 3 (commits are based on developer tasks and user stories): It is evident that in Iteration 1, we did not utilize GitLab that well, but we did better in Iteration 2. In further investigation, it is evident that we closed most of the tasks on the same day of the Iteration 2 due date. It can also be noticed that in iteration 2 and 3, we mostly achieved what we expected, which means our estimations got much better. This was mostly due to reflecting the best of our capabilities on what we had after iteration 1. Also, Iteration 1 was a little overwhelming for all of us since that was a time when we all were trying to learn new environments while maintaining the documentation about the project so we were not able to manage all the workload properly. But we learned and performed accordingly in the next 2 iterations. Also, since, iteration 3 was our last submission we mostly tried to wrap up our project as much as we can and by this time since it was almost 3 months of us working together we tried our best to utilize each team member’s strengths to best of our capabilities.
